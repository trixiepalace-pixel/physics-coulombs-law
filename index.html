PHYSICS: COULOMB’S LAW

Introduction

Coulomb’s Law describes the force between two charged objects. It is a fundamental principle in electrostatics, explaining how electric charges attract or repel each other.

Coulomb's law states that the force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Formula

F = k (q1 × q2) / r²

Where:

  • F = electric force (Newton, N)
  • q1, q2 = charges (Coulombs, C)
  • r = distance between charges (meters, m)
  • k = Coulomb’s constant ≈ 8.99 × 10⁹ Nm²/C²

Key Points

  • Like charges repel each other
  • Unlike charges attract each other
  • Force increases with larger charges
  • Force decreases rapidly as distance increases (inverse square law)

Example

If two charges are placed close together, they experience a strong force. If the distance is doubled, the force becomes one-fourth of the original value.
